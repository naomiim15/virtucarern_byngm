What is Simulation?

Nursing simulation is an educational technique that uses technology and real-life clinical settings to help students learn in a safe, controlled environment. It helps bridge the gap between theory and practice, improving patient outcomes by enabling hands-on, error-based learning before nurses enter real clinical situations. Simulations can include full-body manikins, standardized patients, computer-based cases, and virtual reality modules. This method enhances critical thinking, communication, prioritization, and patient safety awareness — all key components of effective nursing practice.
